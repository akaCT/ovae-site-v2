02 The methodology

The AI-native foundation underneath whichever methodology runs your team.

Orectic is how a company stops running on people's heads and starts running on a system. The sequence is non-negotiable: Company Brain first. Visibility second. Agentic automation third. Scale after validation. It captures institutional knowledge into a permissioned Evidence Core, gives leadership a single trusted source of intelligence, validates the highest-value workflows by hand before automating them, and trains the team to own the system without us. Every company sits in one of four maturity phases; the method meets it where it is. The engine doesn't change between customers — that's the compounding asset.
02 / a Four phases of AI-Native
PHASE 00
Manual / Blind
The company runs on people's heads and disconnected tools.
  • Data scattered across tools — no single source of truth
  • No working dashboard leadership trusts
  • AI used ad hoc by individuals
  • Planning is reactive
PHASE 01
Visible / Instrumented
The company can see itself. BI exists; AI is a tool, not a system.
  • A working dashboard, referenced in real time
  • Core metrics auto-consolidated
  • AI used as point tools by part of the team
  • Operating cadence forming
PHASE 02
Automated / Leveraged
Validated workflows automated. Agents do real work; team supervises.
  • Multiple real workflows under supervised automation
  • Each automation validated by hand first
  • Automation treated as a maintained system
PHASE 03
AI-Native
A human runs the system; the system runs the company.
  • Plan, sense, act on one connected loop
  • New projects scoped AI-native by default
  • Humans are provably not the bottleneck
  • Team owns and evolves the system

02 / d Engagement cadence — how the Embed actually runs

4 phases inside a 120-day Embed. Each ends with a verifiable deliverable.

Front-loaded by design. The Phase Two retainer is left unpriced at close, scoped around Day 90 once the highest-leverage next move is obvious.

Days 0–10
Onboarding & Evidence Core live

End-of-period deliverable — the Company Brain is live and conversational, returning answers with source references.

  • Implementation charter, decision / risk / access logs, baseline KPIs established
  • Tech-stack audit across tools, departments, roles, workflows
  • Evidence Core architecture and permission model deployed
  • Priority ingestion begins under managed onboarding
Days 10–30
Founder judgment & first agents

End-of-period deliverable — a working operating layer, not a pile of disconnected AI notes.

  • Founder OS capture — offer logic, objection handling, coaching principles, decision rules
  • Structured extraction surfaces workflow candidates
  • First executive dashboard, scorecards, adoption tracker, KPI baselines
  • First internal agents and automation-stack audit
Days 30–75
Workflow rollout & operating intelligence

End-of-period deliverable — workflows, dashboards, and agents active enough to evaluate against real company use.

  • Production workflow rollouts across named first-wave departments
  • Scorecards and weekly operating rhythm installed; KPIs live
  • Agentic automation complexity registry created and ranked
  • Capacity / revenue leverage findings and student-layer boundaries formalized
Days 75–120
Leverage, hardening & handoff

End-of-period deliverable — a governed operating layer, a working Evidence Core, production workflows, instrumented dashboards, and a leverage roadmap.

  • Agentic automation priorities implemented where evidence supports them
  • Capacity and revenue leverage roadmap finalized
  • System hardening, observability, approval tiers tuned
  • Adoption verification; student-layer coordination rules completed

The sequencing rule. Company Brain first. Visibility second. Agentic automation third. The order isn't a preference — automation built on opinion inherits the opinion.

09 / b Why the launch IS the publicity moment
Move 01  ·  the product reveal
The launch event itself is the demo.

When The Brain ships, the launch is the show. Live on stage, a real founder signs up. In sixty minutes they walk off with a working dashboard, a documented plan, and their first automations running. The audience sees it happen in real time.

Press, clips and shares fall out of this organically — because the thing is actually useful, not a polished pitch loop.

Move 02  ·  self-referential proof
Built by Ovae, on Ovae.

Ovae's own internal setup runs on The Brain. The firm uses the product to operate itself, and the dashboard is shown publicly. The four arms, the bench, the pipeline — all instrumented inside the box.

The most credible testimonial possible is the operator who built the thing also runs on it.

Move 03  ·  positive viral, not hype viral
Users share it because they love it. Not because they were hyped into trying it.

Same goal — shareability — two different bets on what makes it last.

Polsia model
Rev-share hype + PR stunt
  • Bottom-rung pricing with a 20% rev-share tail
  • PR stunt designed for the install spike
  • Underlying UX that doesn't deliver on the promise
  • $50/mo + 20% revenue share — forever. Growth pressure creates churn; AI-built businesses fail and the share is gone.
Result: high K-factor, low product love. Users churn quickly. Viral but fragile.
vs
Ovae model
Flat-price product, designed to be loved
  • Flat one-time + monthly price, no rev-share
  • Real outcomes inside the first session — dashboard, plan, automations
  • Methodology distributed at scale, not just a tool
  • Free → low-cost subscription → custom install at value. Users own their outcome. Our economics scale with their success, not parasitically off their revenue.
Result: organic word-of-mouth. Viral because it works.
