How the math works. Holding lead flow constant, close rate is a direct multiplier on revenue: new revenue = current × (target ÷ today). The extra revenue still carries ~50% material+labor, but needs no new marketing and almost no new overhead — so it drops to the bottom line at a much higher margin than the average job (the "margin on new revenue" slider). EBITDA lift × your exit multiple = enterprise value created.

Caveats to keep it honest: the $2,000 avg ticket is for service calls — big installs distort exact job counts, which is why this leans on the close-rate ratio (true regardless of ticket mix). Capacity exists today (8 plumbers, idle mornings); past capacity, added techs sit inside the 50% COGS so the model holds.
