02The problem

Most founders are doing one of two things with AI. Both end the same way.

Path A

Do nothing. Cancel your second Saturday in a row. Tell yourself this quarter is the last one like this. Watch the team that built it with you start to leave. The ceiling isn't the market — it's you, and you know it.

Path B

Hire an AI agency. $80K in. Four months of agents nobody on the team understands. No dashboard to audit what they're doing. A renewal landing on your desk. Six months later you're back to Path A — same ceiling, less cash.

The real problem isn't a missing tool. It's a missing operating system.

09FAQ

What founders ask before they sign.

Does this replace my team?
No. Ovae installs the operating layer your team runs on — dashboards, automations, planning cadence. People become more leveraged, not redundant. The bottleneck moves off the humans and into the system.
How is this different from hiring an AI consultant?
We embed as operator and builder for a fixed window, leave you AI-native, and the product keeps working after we're gone. Most consultants leave you a deck. We leave you a running system.
What if I don't have a methodology in place?
Use Ovae Native — our default planning ladder (10-year vision → 3-year picture → 1-year plan → quarterly rocks → weekly cadence). Or adopt one of the supported frameworks during the workshop. Either way, you walk out with documented operating rhythm.
What if my business is just an idea?
Spark is built for that. You can sign up with no revenue and walk away with a documented plan, a starter dashboard, and five automations ready to extend as the business grows.
What if my industry is unusual?
Industry-agnostic by design. The dashboard, automations, and methodology adapt to your specifics. We've worked across coaching, trades, DTC, and more — the operating layer is universal, the configuration is yours.
How fast is fast?
Spark: a working AI-native operating layer in under a day. Build: 6 weeks. Embed: 120 days, fully embedded — Brain by Day 10, dashboards by Day 30, production workflows by Day 75, governed handoff by Day 120. No "let's revisit in Q3" timelines.
What happens after you leave?
You keep the Evidence Core (your Company Brain), the dashboards, the workflows, and the agents — all governed under human-review rules your team owns. The handoff is the deliverable; the engagement completes against a named list, not a calendar date. An optional managed retainer is available if you want us to keep operating it with you.
How do you make sure my team actually uses what you build?
Adoption is instrumented — we install an adoption tracker on Day 30 alongside the first dashboard, and the operating cadence runs weekly while we're embedded. If a workflow isn't being used, we see it and either fix the tool or fix the rhythm. The team that runs it after us is the same team that helped shape it during.
The AI stack changes monthly — won't this be obsolete in six months?
The Evidence Core, the dashboards, and the workflows are model-agnostic by construction. The agentic layer runs as a harness that swaps the underlying model when something better ships. What stays durable is the captured judgment, the visibility, and the governance rules — those don't depreciate when GPT-N+1 arrives.
What if our current process is a mess — won't you just automate it?
That's why Company Brain first, Visibility second, Automation third is non-negotiable. We make the business legible before we touch a workflow. Half the time we end up removing work that didn't deserve to exist instead of automating it. The Evidence Core surfaces the real shape; you decide what's worth keeping.
What if it doesn't work for us?
Spark is cancel-anytime — the install stays yours and there's no clawback. Build has a 14-day refund window if the cohort isn't landing. Embed includes an explicit Day-30 exit clause: by the 30-day milestone you'll have the Company Brain and first dashboards in production — if the work isn't on trajectory by then, we end clean. Completion is tied to a named deliverable list, not the calendar. The risk asymmetry is intentional.
I don't think of myself as "AI-native" — am I the right fit?
Yes — and that's exactly the point. AI-native is the end-state, not the prerequisite. The founders who get the most out of Ovae are operators who've built something real on grit and judgment and want a system that compounds what they already know — not technologists looking for another tool. If you can articulate one workflow that's eating your Tuesdays, you're ready.
