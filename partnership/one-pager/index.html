Ovae × Orectic — The Business, in Brief

For Isaiah & CT · May 2026 · a working one-pager, not a binding agreement

The one line

Orectic is the product. Ovae is the team. Isaiah builds the AI-Native Operating System; Ovae is the official team that brings in clients across verticals, installs it, and runs them — co-branded "Powered by Orectic." One HoldCo owns both, and the asset that compounds — the vertical data + runbooks the clients generate — is owned by the HoldCo, so we both own what we build.

Why now (the opening is real, not hypothetical)

  • Demand is inbound and hot — ~23 discovery calls last week, calendar filling with little marketing.
  • A flagship is already in build — Krista at $175K (25× a normal deal), plus ~$300K of near pipeline and ~$500K projected in the three months after; ~$0.5–1M contracted by the time her engagement closes.
  • The market gap is the implementation layer: non-technical owners can't get from AI hype to AI-native operations. That's exactly what we install.

We capitalize on the attention now, build the data moat with every client, and bootstrap to ~$10M ARR before raising a dollar.

The moat (confidential — this is the "why we win," not for the public site)

The base runtime is open-source (MIT/Hermes) and commoditizable — that's a cost advantage, not the moat. The moat is co-created and compounds:

  • Vertical data + runbooks. Every engagement deposits de-identified operational data and battle-tested runbooks into a HoldCo-owned, governed repository. Engagement #5 in a vertical is cheaper, faster, and more likely to succeed than #1 — and we cross-pollinate what works from one vertical into another (a "crossover score" no single-vertical competitor can see).
  • Expert playbooks + the transformation layer. Our people encode what actually works; the product turns it into a repeatable install. We borrow what works from the coaching world (the founder's identity shift to running an AI-native company) and apply it to a service-and-software business. That blend is the delta.
  • It only stays a moat if it's constructed deliberately — governed/versioned runbook repo, an explicit delivery→product feedback loop, and client contracts that grant us de-identified data rights. We build those from day one.

The honest investor answer to "can't someone fork Hermes and copy you?": They can copy the software. They cannot copy 18 months of co-created vertical runbooks, the client relationships that produced them, or the expansion revenue that proves the moat is real.

How we scale (and how we free Isaiah)

  • Productize the install into a SKU. A standard 90-day install playbook (Discovery → Data Audit → Evidence Core → Dashboards → Runbook co-creation), 80% pre-built per vertical, auto-provisioned by Orectic on signature.
  • Certified "Orectic Implementer" program — Isaiah trains a cohort once; a pool of vetted contract builders runs installs from the playbook. Critical: without this by ~month 4, throughput caps near ~$3M ARR because Isaiah is the bottleneck.
  • Dogfood Orectic on ourselves. Run Ovae's own acquisition → onboarding → delivery on Orectic; publish a live "Ovae runs on Orectic" dashboard. Every demo is our real operation, not a sandbox — the proof no competitor can fake.
  • Funnel: 30-min qualify → paid discovery workshop ($7.5–10K, ~80% convert to flagship) → proposal in 5 days → 50% deposit on signature (self-funds delivery + hiring) → 25% at Evidence Core live → 25% at completion.

Path to $10M ARR (blended, ~12–15 mo): ~20 flagship Embeds (~$220K) + ~20 Builds (~$90K) + a self-serve "Ovae in a Box" tier + retainers ≈ $10M at ~70% blended gross margin. Realistic close rate ramps 1–2 flagships/mo early → 3–4/mo by month ~10 as case studies compound.

Headcount arc: ~$2M ARR: AE + setter + implementation lead + 2 contract builders (Isaiah on escalations only). $5M: add AE, delivery lead, CSM, FT builder. $10M: VP Sales, more AEs, solutions engineer, implementation specialists, head of CS, a RevOps analyst running Orectic on ourselves.

Fundraising — from strength, for leverage and liquidity (not survival)

  • Bootstrap to ~$10M ARR first. At Isaiah's own ~25× reference for this space, that's ~$250M; more conservatively, a data-moat AI company at $10M ARR is ~$100–200M (≈$150M achievable with clean metrics + >120% net revenue retention).
  • Then a strategic round we don't need — e.g., ~$20–25M total: ~$15M primary (~10% dilution) + ~$10M secondary so we each take ~$5M+ off the table while keeping control (the model Ben used: big check, small dilution, partial liquidity). Lead with a top financial investor; add one strategic as a co-investor with information rights, no board seat, to preserve optionality.
  • Personal targets stay intact and aligned: CT ~$5M near-term breathing room → $20–30M; Isaiah $30–50M generational — both on the same curve, then keep compounding toward a much larger outcome. $60–80M is a milestone, not the ceiling.

Protect it (do this in tandem with selling — be data-room-ready)

  • Before the next client signs: IP assignment (all founders + contractors), founder agreement + vesting, Orectic contribution agreement, and a client MSA with the data-rights + runbook-ownership clauses. Don't take a paying client without these.
  • Contractor-heavy delivery → tight controls: explicit IP-assignment in every contractor agreement, NDAs with vertical carve-outs, tiered access (a contractor sees only their client), runbooks in company-controlled infra with audit logs, same-day offboarding, non-solicit.
  • Client data rights: perpetual license to use de-identified, aggregated data for benchmarking/product improvement; frame it to clients as "contributing to the industry benchmark your competitors will envy." Pursue SOC 2 early — it's what makes clients sign without a fight and what investors diligence.
  • Build the "moat memo" + reference customers now — the two things that turn $10M ARR into a premium multiple.

Mutual protection (so neither of us gets burned)

  • For CT (no Opus repeat): equity not commission; anti-dilution floor; equal board seat (you / Isaiah / one independent); pro-rata rights; defined role with acceleration if removed without cause; pay as salary, not a draw.
  • For Isaiah: a milestone-linked tranche of CT's grant (tied to fair GTM targets) with a cure period; reverse-vesting if CT exits early; brand-quality control over the "Powered by Orectic" mark; protection from being dragged back into services.

Where we focus — verticals (depth beats breadth; the moat compounds per vertical)

Go deep on two first: 1. High-revenue coaches, masterminds & creator-educators. Krista is live proof ($175K), and her mastermind is a room of identical buyers — formalize her as a paid referral partner now. Fast single-call yeses; playbooks repeat client-to-client (launch sequences, community onboarding, call summarization, testimonial capture, referral triggers). Pitch: "Scale your program without scaling your time — AI-native in 90 days." 2. Agencies & professional-services firms ($3–20M revenue). The founder/managing-partner is the bottleneck and feels it; urgency is structural (AI-native boutiques underbid them on margin). Enter via a $15–25K audit → $125–175K install. Playbooks compound across sub-types (client reporting, proposals, retainer renewal, knowledge capture). Pitch: "Grow revenue without growing headcount — starting with a 2-week audit."

Everything else = opportunistic (home services/trades, etc.): serve at audit / Phase-1 only, stretch existing runbooks, and never build a new category-specific runbook for an opportunistic client. If 3+ closed clients emerge organically in a category, promote it to a focus vertical. This is how we serve anyone without diluting the moat.
