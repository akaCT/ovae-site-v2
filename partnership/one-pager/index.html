Ovae × Orectic — The Business, in Brief

For Isaiah & CT · May 2026 · Confidential working draft — not a binding agreement

The one line

Orectic is the product. Ovae is the team. Isaiah builds the AI-Native Operating System; Ovae is the single front door that signs clients across verticals, installs the platform, and runs the transformation — co-branded "Powered by Orectic." One HoldCo owns both. The asset that compounds — the vertical data and runbooks every engagement generates — lives at the HoldCo parent, so we both own what we build together.

Why now

  • Demand is inbound and moving fast — ~23 discovery calls in a single week, calendar filling with minimal marketing.
  • A flagship is already in build — Krista at $175K (25× a standard deal), plus ~$300K of near pipeline and ~$500K projected in the following three months; ~$0.5–1M contracted before her engagement closes.
  • The market gap is the implementation layer: non-technical owners can't bridge AI hype to AI-native operations. That is exactly what we install.

We capitalize on this attention now, build the moat — the co-created runbooks and operating depth — with every client, and bootstrap to ~$10M ARR before raising a dollar.

The moat

Confidential — the "why we win," not for the public site.

The base runtime is open-source (MIT/Hermes) and commoditizable — a cost advantage, not the moat. The moat is co-created and compounds:

  • Co-created runbooks + playbooks. Every engagement deposits battle-tested runbooks and configurations into a HoldCo-owned, governed repository. Engagement #5 in a vertical is cheaper, faster, and higher-probability than #1 — and the cross-pollination of what works across verticals is something no single-vertical competitor can see. (The codified playbooks and the operating position are the asset; raw client data on its own doesn't compound into a moat.)
  • Expert playbooks + the transformation layer. Our people encode what actually works; the product turns it into a repeatable install. We borrow the identity-shift frameworks from the coaching world and apply them to a service-and-software business. That blend is the delta.
  • It only stays a moat if built deliberately — a governed, versioned runbook repo; an explicit delivery→product feedback loop; and client contracts that grant us de-identified data rights. All three go in from day one.

The honest investor answer to "can't someone fork Hermes and copy you?": They can copy the software. They cannot copy 18 months of co-created vertical runbooks, the client relationships that produced them, or the expansion revenue that proves the moat is real.

The lanes

It's one company, and it employs everyone — the engineers who build the product and the team that signs and runs clients. The split isn't two payrolls; it's who leads what:

  • Isaiah → product & authority. Orectic's roadmap, infrastructure, and architecture; building his personal brand as the recognized authority in the space. Off repeatable installs by ~month 6.
  • CT → go-to-market & ecosystem. Brand, sales engine, onboarding experience, events, relationships, talent, and building both founders' public profiles together.
  • Both are fluid across CEO / product / operator roles; both build. The split reflects the current reality — GTM is the scarce, high-leverage constraint.

How we scale (and how we free Isaiah)

  • Productize the install into a SKU. A standard 90-day playbook (Discovery → Data Audit → Evidence Core → Dashboards → Runbook Co-creation), 80% pre-built per vertical, auto-provisioned by Orectic on signature.
  • Certified "Orectic Implementer" program — Isaiah trains a cohort once; a pool of vetted contract builders runs installs from the playbook. Without this by ~month 4, throughput caps near ~$3M ARR with Isaiah as the bottleneck.
  • Dogfood Orectic on ourselves. Run Ovae's own acquisition → onboarding → delivery loop on Orectic; publish a live "Ovae runs on Orectic" dashboard. Every demo is our real operation, not a sandbox — proof no competitor can fake.
  • Funnel: 30-min qualify → paid discovery workshop ($7.5–10K, ~80% convert to flagship) → proposal in 5 days → 50% deposit on signature (self-funds delivery and first hires) → 25% at Evidence Core live → 25% at completion.

Path to $10M ARR (blended, ~12–15 months): ~30 flagship Embeds (~$220K) + ~25 Builds (~$90K) + a self-serve tier (The Brain) + retainers ≈ $10M at ~62–70% blended gross margin. Close rate ramps 1–2 flagships/mo early → 3–4/mo by month ~10 as case studies compound.

Fundraising — from strength, for leverage and liquidity

  • Bootstrap to ~$10M ARR first. At the ~25× reference comparable Isaiah cited, that's ~$250M; conservatively, a data-moat AI company at $10M ARR with clean metrics and >120% net revenue retention is ~$100–200M (≈$150M achievable).
  • Then a strategic round we don't need — e.g., ~$20–25M total: ~$15M primary (~10% dilution) + ~$10M secondary so we each take ~$5M+ off the table while keeping control (the Ben model: large check, low dilution, partial liquidity). Lead with a top financial investor; one strategic co-investor with information rights and no board seat, to preserve optionality.
  • Personal targets stay aligned: CT ~$5M near-term breathing room → $20–30M; Isaiah $30–50M generational — both on the same curve, compounding toward a much larger outcome. $60–80M is a milestone, not the ceiling.

Protect it

Build the data room in parallel with signing clients — be ready.

  • Before the next client signs: IP assignment (all founders and contractors), founder agreement + vesting, Orectic contribution agreement, and a client MSA with data-rights + runbook-ownership clauses. No paying client without these in place.
  • Contractor-heavy delivery → tight controls: explicit IP assignment in every contractor agreement; NDAs with vertical carve-outs; tiered access (each contractor sees only their client); runbooks in company-controlled infrastructure with audit logs; same-day offboarding; non-solicit.
  • Client data rights: perpetual license to use de-identified, aggregated data for benchmarking and product improvement — framed to clients as "contributing to the industry benchmark your competitors will envy." Pursue SOC 2 early; it's what gets clients to sign without friction and what investors diligence.
  • Build the "moat memo" + reference customers now — the two things that turn $10M ARR into a premium multiple.

Mutual protection

The clean break — each keeps their lane. The thing that makes it safe enough for both to start: if it ends, neither is stranded. - Orectic reverts to Isaiah — the product and IP he built return to him (contribution + dormant-license terms). - Ovae stays with CT — the brand, go-to-market, client relationships, and team; and because the base is open-source, CT can rebuild the product layer with another technical partner. Both walk in with a real lane and a real alternative path. - The co-created moat (data + runbooks) is shared on a split — each gets a perpetual license to the generalized playbooks; clients follow Ovae.

  • For CT: equity, not commission; anti-dilution floor; equal board seat (CT / Isaiah / one independent); pro-rata rights; defined role with acceleration if removed without cause; salary, not a draw. Beyond equity: the C-corp shields personal liability, Isaiah warrants the contributed Orectic IP is clean (licensed OSS, no infringement), and the company carries D&O/E&O insurance.
  • The Opus wall: CT's only outside exposure is one personal guarantee (~$60K to settle). The separate C-corp + transfer-restrictions wall NewCo from it; Opus's other liabilities stayed in the Opus entity (assets sold to Superconductor Labs, obligations not assumed); CT settles the guarantee personally from normal comp. Two ~30-min counsel confirmations before signing (no officer-liability; guarantee is the ceiling).
  • For Isaiah: the Orectic reversion above (the core "it stays his"); reverse-vesting/clawback if CT exits early; role definition with material-non-performance defined; CT warrants no undisclosed outside liabilities enter the company (the Opus wall); brand-quality control over the "Powered by Orectic" mark. (This — plus CT's vesting and the clean break — replaces the earlier milestone tranche.)

Verticals — depth beats breadth

The moat compounds per vertical. Go deep on two first.

1. High-revenue coaches, masterminds & creator-educators. Krista is live proof ($175K), and her mastermind is a room of identical buyers — formalize her as a paid referral partner now. Fast single-call closes; playbooks repeat client-to-client (launch sequences, community onboarding, call summarization, testimonial capture, referral triggers). Pitch: "Scale your program without scaling your time — AI-native in 90 days."

2. Agencies & professional-services firms ($3–20M revenue). The founder or managing partner is the bottleneck and knows it; urgency is structural (AI-native boutiques are underbidding them on margin). Enter via a $15–25K audit → $125–175K install. Playbooks compound across sub-types (client reporting, proposals, retainer renewal, knowledge capture). Pitch: "Grow revenue without growing headcount — starting with a 2-week audit."

Everything else = opportunistic (home services, trades, etc.): serve at audit or Phase 1 only, stretch existing runbooks, and never build a new category-specific runbook for an opportunistic client. If 3+ closed clients emerge organically in a category, promote it to a focus vertical. This is how we serve broadly without diluting the moat.
