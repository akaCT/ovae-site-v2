04Delivery Model — Who Does What, What It Costs

The clarity you wanted: the journey, the time, the "build materials."

The ascension ladder (every client)

  • Audit — consulting; identify automatable bottlenecks. ~10 days calendar / 5–7 hrs founder interviews. Trust-building.
  • Company Brain V1 — MCP-driven chatbot they talk to their business through (Claude app, or Telegram bot for non-technical like Tommy). ~1 day in terminal (Isaiah).
  • Copilot — tool connectors + manual invoke (estimate → invoice → reminder; permit research). Paid automations.
  • Autopilot — bid comes in, everything runs automatically. Top of the ladder.

Division of labor

CTIsaiah
Leads audits (right questions, what's buildable), dashboards/UX, workflow scoping, the relationship/close (Tommy, Brian), lead-finding (builds the list; not dialing)Brain deploy + builds/execution on his platform (fast, governed), Dash MVP, the technical/system pitch

Hard costs (the margin picture)

  • Mostly founder time + tokens (~$200 Claude Max per heavy build). Platform is ~70% automated → pushing 80–90%.
  • First FTE lever: Micah ~$3.5K (or $75/hr; ~20hrs ≈ $2K) to knock out simple deployments → capture the recurring asset, free Isaiah.
  • Capacity: ~2–3 of these $5–20M service-co deals per month at current setup.
Capacity & scaling

Builds run on Isaiah's platform. To take on more than ~2–3 deals/month, stand up a builder (Micah ~$3.5K) for the simple deployments. CT owns audits + dashboards/UX + lead-finding so Isaiah stays focused on the builds — that division is what keeps delivery from bottlenecking as volume grows.
